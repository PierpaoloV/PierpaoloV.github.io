PhD Dissertation · Radboud University Medical Center · 2021 – 2024

Learning from Histopathology Images: AI-Driven Biomarkers for Pancreatic Ductal Adenocarcinoma

PhD thesis focusing on the development of artificial intelligence methods for digital pathology in pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma (PDAC). It addresses major challenges in prognostic assessment through automated tumor segmentation, biomarker quantification, and survival prediction from whole-slide histopathology images. A central contribution is the development of robust pipelines for tumor-stroma ratio analysis and other interpretable morphological biomarkers associated with patient outcome. The work also evaluates multiple deep learning feature extraction strategies for their ability to predict overall survival across different datasets. The findings demonstrate the potential of computational pathology to improve the speed, consistency, and clinical relevance of histopathological assessment in pancreatic cancer. Overall, the thesis contributes scalable and interpretable AI-based tools that support prognostic modeling and future precision oncology research.

PhD Research · Radboud UMC · 2021 – 2024

Automatic Quantification of Tumor-Stroma Ratio as a prognostic biomarker for pancreatic cancer.

Developed an automated whole-slide image analysis pipeline for pancreatic cancer within the EU Horizon 2020 PANCAIM consortium. Using multi-class U-Net models for tissue and tumor segmentation, the method achieved external Dice scores up to 0.86 across a multi-center cohort of 368 patients from 25 Dutch pathology laboratories. The extracted tumor-stroma ratio biomarker showed prognostic value, reaching an AUC of 0.61 for 6-month survival prediction. Tech: PyTorch · MONAI · Docker · SLURM · Python
